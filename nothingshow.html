The project "The Nothing Show" started with the idea to fake generate a topic for an open call group exhibition. For this occasion we were looking for a person to be the actor of a livestream Topic Draw, including a short Video featering a Fake Rapper beeing interviewed by our main guest. After the topic was found, we launched an Open Call with the Theme "Nothing" looking for potencial participants for an exhibition called "The Nothing Show". For some reason the decision was made to design the exhibition not as a classic presentation, but as an unpackaging video
format, taking place on the deck of a floating island on a river. As the whole concept was inspired by "DADA", the next steps couldn´t be more stupid or random. As a final selection, 46 artworks were collected and temporarily installed on a floating boat island on the Danube River, in Vienna. The concept was created around the idea to make a joke about the common made up Holy Spirit around work presentations or the exclusivity of art spaces. This fleeting exhibition existed only for a short time, was fully documented and now remains as a video shaped Exhibition, titled „The Nothing Show“.
Through the wish to bring all these works back together and display them within a space, the artworks started to refer back to the original exhibition on the island and its documentation.
The curatorial decision to present them like relics or traces of an event that has already past, felt like the only Logical Solution and puts the attention back to the video. Which is the main „Show“.
The Nothing Show is one project, splitted in three parts.
